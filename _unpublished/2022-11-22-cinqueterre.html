Milan
1-3 days

description-html: >-

Milan, the bustling heart of Northern Italy, is a city that combines history and modernity with effortless grace. Known as the fashion capital of the world and the home of Italian design, Milan is a city that always keeps you on your toes with its energetic pace. From the iconic Cathedral of Milan to the cutting-edge museums, this city is a feast for the senses. Whether you're looking for haute couture, historical landmarks, or delicious Italian cuisine, Milan has it all. Join us as we delve into the vibrant culture of this city and uncover the hidden gems that make Milan one of the most exciting places to visit in Italy.

left-image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-duomo-3.jpg right-image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-galleria.jpg - _id: recommendations title: Recommendations background-color: '#000000' position: left eat: - name: Obicà Mozzarella Bar link-url: https://www.obica.com/ description-html: >-

This pizzeria is located on the 7th Floor of RinascenteMilan in Piazza Duomo and offers a great view of the Duomo. Great place to start of your trip with some delicious mozzarella and Aperol Spritz.

image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-duomo-1.jpg - name: Riso e Latte link-url: https://risoelatte.com/ description-html: >-

This tiny restaurant is a hidden gem - you will feel likeyou travelled back to Italy in the 60s and it is so lovely.Be sure to make a reservation and check out theadorable bathroom while your there.

image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-riso-latte.jpg - name: Bar Luce link-url: https://www.fondazioneprada.org/barluce-en/ description-html: >-

This cafe is a part of the Fondazione Prada museumand is designed by none other but Wes Anderson.Definitelv a must-see for any fans of his movies.

image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-bar-luce.jpg stay: - name: Combo Milano link-url: www.google.de description-html: >-

The hostel is located in Navigli (read more below), with a lot of bars and restaurants just around the corner.

image: '' see: - name: Duomo & Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II link-url: https://www.duomomilano.it/en/ description-html: >-

The Piazza del Duomo be most likely be the first stopon your sightseeing journey as the Duomo di Milano isthe most famous landmark of the city. You can visit the inside of the Duomo and then catch the gorgeous viewsfrom its famous rooftop. It's 15€ to enter and walk up the stairs and 20€ for the elevator.

Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II is just to the left side ofthe Duomo. It is Italy's oldest active shopping galleryand stands out due to its gorgeous architecture. Theshops might be slightly out of budget, but I was morefocused on the pretty glass roof anyway.

image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-galleria.jpg - name: Sforza Castle & Parco Sempione link-url: www.google.de description-html: >-

One of the things I like to do while exploring other citiesis take a stroll through one of their parks. After vou'veimmersed yourself in a piece of history of Milan at theSforza Castle you can take a break in the nearby ParcoSempione.

image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-parco-sempione.jpg - name: Cimitero Monumentale link-url: description-html: >-

This may sound a bit weird but the cementary of Milanwas a true highlight of my visit. It was beyond fascinating to see the extraordinary tombs and walking around felt so peaceful.

image: >- /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-cimitero-monumentale.jpg - name: Fondazione Prada link-url: https://www.fondazioneprada.org/ description-html: >-

This is the place to go for all lovers of modern art (andif you want to see the Bar Luce as mentioned above). Avery cool place to spend a few hours. I especially lovedthe exterior of the museum the Carsten Höller's UpsideDown Mushroom Room.

image: >- /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-fondanzione-prada.jpg - name: Navigli link-url: '' description-html: >-

There are endless bars and restaurants to choose from along the canal for a fun night out in Navigli. If you want to come earlier, you can check out the cute shops around this area, like TENOHA, Europe's first Japanese concept store.

image: /assets/images/posts/italy/elsewhere-travel-milan-navigli.jpg - name: Bosco Verticale link-url: '' description-html: >-

I'm a sucker for architecture involving a lot of plants, soI really wanted to see these green buildings. The wholearea around the financial district Porta Nuova is actuallyquite interesting if you like modern architecture.

Bologna
Day 3-5

description-html: >-

Step into the culinary capital of Italy, where every street is a chapter in a delicious story - welcome to Bologna! Known as 'La Grassa' (The Fat One), this city is a tapestry of culture, history, and irresistible flavors. From handmade tagliatelle to aged Parmigiano-Reggiano, Bologna's gastronomic treasures are bound to captivate your senses. Join me as we explore hidden trattorias, wander ancient porticos, and savor every moment in this vibrant city.
